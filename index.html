AI as a Healthcare Substitute Among Uninsured Young Adults

"Are young adults (18–30) using free AI tools as a substitute for healthcare they can't afford, and how does that shape their health decisions?"

An original survey study examining the intersection of healthcare access, cost, and AI adoption in young adults aged 18–30. The study screens for participants who have used AI tools for health-related questions and explores motivations including cost barriers, insurance status, trust levels, and emotional outcomes. A follow-up interview component allows for deeper qualitative insight.

Status: Data collection in progress. Findings and analysis to be added upon completion.

Signal and Soul: AI Usage, Parasocial Bonds, and Human Connection Across Socioeconomic Groups

"As AI becomes embedded in daily life, are people forming emotional bonds with AI — and who pays the highest price?"

A data science and human-centered analysis of 503 respondents examining how AI usage frequency relates to loneliness, human social support, parasocial bonding, and cultural identity across income tiers. Dataset modeled on CDC Household Pulse Survey, Pew Research AI adoption data, and Meta-Gallup Global State of Social Connections. Cleaned and analyzed in Python using Pandas.
